What is Insulin Resistance?

The Hidden Epidemic

Insulin resistance is the most common metabolic disorder, yet it often goes undetected because doctors only measure blood glucose, missing the earliest signs of metabolic dysfunction.

Common Root Cause

Dr. Bikman's research shows insulin resistance as the "common soil" from which most chronic diseases grow - diabetes, obesity, Alzheimer's, heart disease, and cancer.

Cellular Impact

Insulin affects every cell in your body. When cells become resistant, some don't respond enough (causing dysfunction), while others respond too much (causing overstimulation).

The Process of Insulin Resistance

1

Normal State

Insulin efficiently helps glucose enter cells

2

Resistance Begins

Cells become less responsive to insulin's signals

3

Compensation

Pancreas produces more insulin to maintain blood glucose

4

Hidden Danger

Blood glucose stays normal while insulin levels skyrocket

How to Detect Insulin Resistance

Visual Signs

Acanthosis Nigricans

Dark, velvety patches of skin with "crinkled tissue paper" texture, usually around neck, armpits, or groin

Skin Tags

Small, mushroom-like growths, most common around the neck - strong indicator of insulin resistance

Blood Tests

Fasting Insulin

Optimal: ≤6 μU/mL Warning: 15-20 μU/mL Problem: >20 μU/mL

Triglyceride-to-HDL Ratio

Formula: Triglycerides ÷ HDL Optimal: <1.5 Better predictor than LDL for cardiovascular risk

Evidence-Based Solutions

The #1 Intervention

Change Your Breakfast

"The simplest strategy would just be change breakfast tomorrow... The longer the insulin is low, the more you're improving your insulin sensitivity."

Option 1: Skip breakfast (intermittent fasting)
Option 2: Low-insulin breakfast (protein + healthy fats)

Dietary Strategy

Foods to Emphasize

  • Proteins: Eggs, fish, meat, Greek yogurt
  • Fats: Avocado, olive oil, nuts, seeds
  • Vegetables: All non-starchy vegetables
  • Fruits: Berries in moderation

Foods to Minimize

  • Refined carbohydrates: Bread, pasta, rice, sugar
  • Processed foods with added sugars
  • Excessive saturated fats

Meal Timing

Time-Restricted Eating

  • Stop eating 3-4 hours before bed
  • 16:8 intermittent fasting (16 hours fasting, 8 hours eating)
  • Melatonin shuts down insulin production at night

Lifestyle Factors

Exercise

  • Morning exercise may be more insulin-sensitizing
  • Post-meal walks for glucose control
  • Both aerobic and resistance training

Sleep & Stress

  • 7-9 hours of consistent sleep
  • Stress management (cortisol control)
  • Dark environment for melatonin
